India: A Memory

Apr 03, 2015

India is now but a distant memory in my mind, a haze of khaki dusty roads, a slight ray of warmth, the burn of delicious spices in my mouth, the sandy heat of my feet on the sun-warmed rooftops. I remember the jasmine tree outside of my aunt’s house, the delicate flowers sweet-smelling and vibrant white against the rich dark green of the tree, and I remember biting into a soft, ripe mango, the juice dribbling down my chin as the rich sweetness dissolves on the tip of my tongue. I remember the delicate smells, wafting gently from the kitchen, a hint of the delicious curries to come. Although India is now but a distant memory, I still remember the crumbs of the laddus melting on my tongue, and I still remember the flashes of color, the dresses whisking back and forth, radiant flowers amongst the dusty air.
